What Cabinet is

Cabinet is the calm, private home for the paperwork that runs your life. It turns bureaucratic goals like visas, university applications, and tax season into simple document checklists, and keeps every original in one place on your Mac. The brand should always feel the way the app feels: organized, reassuring, and quietly capable.

Identity

Logo & mark

Cabinet
The mark

Two offset drawers, each with a small white handle. It reads as a filing cabinet and echoes the app itself, where every process is a drawer. The mark works alone or locked up with the wordmark.

Construction

Each drawer is built from three stacked layers: a soft shadow at 15% orange, a lighter back drawer in #FB923C, and the front in #EA580C, with a 92% white handle. Never flatten it to a single orange.

Clear space: always leave at least one drawer-handle height of empty space around the mark on every side.

✓ Do

  • Keep the three-layer depth and the white handles
  • Give it clear space on all sides
  • Use the white app-icon container on colored backgrounds
  • Scale the mark and wordmark together

✕ Don't

  • Flatten it to one flat orange or a single drawer
  • Recolor the drawers or drop the handles
  • Rotate, skew, or add effects
  • Crowd it against text or edges

Signature

The drawer motif & UI

Everything is a drawer

The drawer is Cabinet's signature object. Processes are drawers with a front panel and a handle. Papers peek out while documents are still being collected, and tuck away when everything is filed. Use it as illustration, empty states, and hero moments. It is the one shape that is unmistakably Cabinet.

Shape language

Generous, soft corners everywhere: 9 to 16px on cards and buttons, fully rounded on drawers. Nothing sharp. The feel is calm and tactile, like well-made stationery.

Motion

Subtle and physical. Papers rise when you hover a drawer, pills slide between tabs, cards lift gently. One easing curve throughout. Nothing bounces or flashes. Think Apple, not carnival.

Buttons
Primary action Secondary Destructive

Primary is the orange gradient with a soft glow and a shimmer sweep. Secondary is an orange outline. Destructive is the only place red appears. One primary action per view.

Words

Voice & tone

Cabinet talks like a calm, capable friend who is good with paperwork. Warm but not chatty, clear but not cold, confident but never corporate. We reduce anxiety, we never add to it.

🎯
Concrete over abstract

Name the real thing: "passport", "bank statement", "expires in 24 days". Not "assets" or "items".

🌿
Calm and reassuring

The user is often stressed about a deadline. Speak plainly and take the pressure off.

✂️
Short and human

Say it in the fewest warm words. Cut filler. A sentence a friend would actually say.

✓ Sounds like Cabinet

"Know exactly what is still missing."

"Your documents never leave your Mac."

"Expiry dates never sneak up on you again."

✕ Not Cabinet

"Leverage our document management solution."

"Securely sync your assets to the cloud."

"Optimize your compliance workflow today!"

The one hard rule

Never use dash characters in UI copy or labels. No hyphens as connectors, no em-dashes. Rephrase with commas, periods, or shorter sentences. This keeps the interface clean and is a firm Cabinet convention.
